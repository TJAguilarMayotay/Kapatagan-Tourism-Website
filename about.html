Municipal History

Kapatagan, which means "plain area" in the local dialect, was officially created as a municipality on June 22, 1959, under Executive Order No. 350. Originally inhabited by the Maranao people, the area gradually saw migration from neighboring provinces, creating a diverse cultural tapestry.

The town played a significant role in the agricultural development of Lanao del Norte, with its vast plains becoming the region's breadbasket. During the American colonial period, Kapatagan served as an important trading post between the coastal and inland communities.

Today, Kapatagan stands as a thriving municipality that balances agricultural heritage with modern development, while preserving its unique cultural identity and natural resources.

Quick Facts

Etymology

Kapatagan comes from the Visayan word meaning "plain" or "flat land", referring to the municipality's vast plains.

Geography

Located in the southern part of Lanao del Norte, bounded by Sultan Naga Dimaporo to the north and Kauswagan to the east.

Topography

Characterized by flat to gently rolling terrain with elevation ranging from 50 to 300 meters above sea level.

Barangays

Composed of 33 barangays, with Poblacion as the town center and administrative capital.
